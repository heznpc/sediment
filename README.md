**Sediment: How Timestamp Visibility and Streak Mechanics Drive Compulsive Production of Low-Quality Digital Content**

Rivers carry fine particles that settle into layers of sediment — individually insignificant, collectively they bury the riverbed and reshape the landscape. Similarly, platform timestamp mechanics (streak counters, contribution graphs, daily challenge logs) compel users to produce meaningless filler content that accumulates as digital sediment, burying meaningful information and degrading platform ecosystems.

## Key Claim

Timestamp visibility and streak mechanics function as a **demand-side driver of digital slop**. Existing research focuses on the supply side (AI makes low-quality content cheap to produce), but overlooks the behavioral compulsion: platform UIs that visualize temporal continuity (GitHub green squares, Duolingo streaks, 1일 1커밋 challenges) create gap-anxiety that users resolve by producing dummy content. When AI tools are available, this compulsion is offloaded to AI — closing the loop between demand-side streak mechanics and supply-side AI generation, systematically producing slop at scale.
